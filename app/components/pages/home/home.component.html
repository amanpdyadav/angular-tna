A nonprofit organization with the aim of organizing cultural and social events to promote Nepal and Nepalese community

In 2004 there were only four Nepalese who lived in Turku; out of those, two were students. Since then, the number of Nepalese residing in Turku has been gradually increasing. Today, the Nepalese community has over a 100 people, from entrepreneurs and restaurateurs to undergraduate and PhD students.

Turku Nepal Association (TNA) was established in 2014 as a nonprofit organization with the aim of organizing cultural and social events to promote Nepal and Nepalese community residing in Turku, Finland.

With the increase in the number of people, we saw a need for an association that would bring all of us together in times of celebration as well as in times of need.

The association supports members of Nepali community in Turku and provides information about Nepal as a place to visit and do business in. In addition, the association raises funds for operations and development activities for the Nepali community as well as communities in Nepal.
